My name is Masanari Kondo (JPN: 近藤 将成). I am currently an assistant professor in the <a href="https://posl.ait.kyushu-u.ac.jp/index.html">Principles of Software engineering and programming Languages Lab. (POSL)</a> at Kyushu University, Kyushu, Japan (2021.4-). I was a Ph.D. student in the <a href="https://se.is.kit.ac.jp/">Software Engineering Laboratory (SEL)</a> at the Kyoto Institute of Technology, Kyoto, Japan under the supervision of prof. <a href="https://se.is.kit.ac.jp/~o-mizuno/">Osamu Mizuno</a>. I was also a Young Scientist of Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) Research Fellowships (DC1) (2019.4-2021.3). I was a visiting researcher in the <a href="https://sail.cs.queensu.ca/">Software Analysis & Intelligence Lab (SAIL)</a> at Queen's University, Kingston, Canada under the supervision of prof. <a href="https://research.cs.queensu.ca/home/ahmed/home/">Ahmed E. Hassan</a> and a Young Ambassador in the "TOBITATE!" program (2018.3-2019.3). My research interests include supporting software developers by providing tools and methods for software quality assurance activities based on software repository mining, machine learning, and statistical analysis techniques. I received my BSc, MSc, and Ph.D. degrees from the Kyoto Institute of Technology (2017, 2019, and 2021). More about my work can be read on my <a href="https://mkmknd.github.io/publications/">publications</a>.
